Variations

I always find myself better at thinking discursively and musically more luid when I have a book in my hand or another compsoer's score in front of me. My creativity is intertextual. One of the things that has stuck to my mind from the few years of working on Wittgenstein during my MA in philosophy, is his description of his own originality: “My originality (if that is the right word) is, I believe, an originality that belongs to the soil, not the seed. (Perhaps I have no seed of my own.) Sow a seed in my soil and it will grow differently than it would in any other soil.”

I don’t know much about the philosophy of originality or creativity (is there a difference between them?) but I don’t know if there is another sort of originality, at least not in my experience: I start thinking ‘originally’ only after I read a few lines from a book or remember the ideas I’ve heard or read somewhere; I start improvising ‘originally’ only when I engage with some pre-given materials. In this respect, I have always found myself an intertextual person. And I though I could devote this page to this inherent intertextuality in me. I will quote ‘seeds’ from others and use them in my soil to—hopefully—offer an original thing or at least a new interpretation of the given material.
